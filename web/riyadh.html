Restaurants & Cafes

La Brasserie

La Brasserie’s spread of international plates dished out in an upscale and modern environment in the Al Faisaliah Hotel set the standard. Every detail appears to be thought of with consideration from the interactive stations to the oh-so-tempting displays.

Al Orjouan

The all-day dining restaurant understands what makes a brilliant buffet: variety, atmosphere, something for all ages and ambience. The experience has all of that in spades, including a great mix of international and Middle Eastern flavours in a venue that will certainly not go unnoticed.

Signor sassi

Villa Signor Sassi welcomes guests to a truly unique fine-dining experience. Signor Sassi embraces its guests with genuine Italian hospitality and takes them on a culinary journey through its exceptional contemporary Italian cuisine.

Le maschou

Fine dining symbol of rich culinary flavours and a sophisticated ambience from the old alleys of France. We pursue this by offering our guests a unique and one-of-a-kind dining experience, an exceptionally authentic array of our wood-fire smoked meats will leave customers always craving for more.

Malmoum Cafe

Malmoum Cafe just opened its doors in Riyadh’s Lakum Artspace. The cafe has actual and latte art too. The venue is spacious and has calming vibes, head here for an evening full of art and coffee.

Fabrica de Cafe

Portal to the Mediterranean with neutral tones, warm light, ceramic vases, and cosy corners carved out of the walls. The cafe offers classic French pastries, chunky cookies, and ice cream alongside a range of hot and cold drinks.

Moon Shell Cafe

Moon Shell cafe is one of the few exclusively vegan/vegetarian cafes in Riyadh. Its health-conscious menu is complemented by a distinctly bohemian look and feel.

Elixir Bunn

awarded ‘Best Design in the Hospitality Sector’ by Architectural Digest Middle East, and for good reason. From terrazzo flooring to grand arches to a winding stairway in an unexpected blue, the cafe is a sight to behold.

ECRU Cafe

Ecru comes from the French word écru, which means “unbleached.”.this spot seems to be perfect for a sunset coffee trip. Sip on lattes, or its signature blondey latte while you marvel at the city ahead.



Hotels

Braira Hotel

The rooms are fitted with a flat-screen TV. Certain rooms have a seating area for your convenience. Every room is equipped with a private bathroom fitted with a shower and bidet only. There is a 24-hour front desk at the property.

Hilton Hotel

Hilton Riyadh Hotel & Residences provides experiences for business & leisure guests alike. Our hotel offers stylish guest rooms, wide range of services, spacious multi-purpose meetings facilities, & world-class dining options.

Ritz Carlton

Among the most majestic five-star hotels in Saudi Arabia, The Ritz-Carlton, Riyadh, takes guests on a captivating journey throughout its 52 acres of lavishly landscaped gardens, spacious and sumptuous accommodations, fine-dining restaurants and 62.000 square feet of elegant event space.

Fairmont

The new hotel forms a key component of the Business Gate community in Riyadh, a mixed-use development project and business district featuring over 70 multinational and regional companies.

Hyatt Regency

Hyatt Regency Riyadh Olaya is located in the heart of the capital, next to the vibrant Tahlia Street and close to commercial and retail districts such as the financial district, Riyadh International Convention & Exhibition Centre and its only 35Km from King Khalid International Airport.

Rosh Rayhaan

Located in the heart of Riyadh, the 5-star Rosh Rayhaan by Rotana offers 236 splendid rooms and suites ranging from classic rooms to royal suites in addition to the Club Rotana rooms that specifically meet the needs of a business traveler.
